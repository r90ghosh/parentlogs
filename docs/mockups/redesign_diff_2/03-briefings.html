Baby Development

Your baby's face is now almost fully formed. The inner ear structure — responsible for hearing and balance — has developed to the point where your baby can detect sounds outside the womb. You may notice your partner reacting when you play music near her belly. The lung branches are growing rapidly, though the lungs themselves won't be ready to breathe air until closer to week 36. Your baby is also developing a layer of fat, giving their skin a smoother appearance.

This week also marks an important milestone for brain development. The cerebellum — the part of the brain that coordinates muscle control and balance — is growing fast. Your baby spends much of their time in REM sleep, which is essential for this neural development.

Her Experience
Glucose Tolerance Test Due

Typically scheduled between weeks 24–28. A routine screening — help her remember to fast beforehand if required.

Increased Fatigue

The extra weight and interrupted sleep may leave her more tired than usual. Small gestures — a warm tea, taking a task off her list — go further than you think.

Braxton Hicks Contractions

Practice contractions may begin. These are normal and irregular — but worth discussing with her midwife if they become frequent or painful.

Back Ache & Posture Changes

Her center of gravity is shifting. Offering a gentle back massage — or suggesting a prenatal pillow — can make evenings much more comfortable.
