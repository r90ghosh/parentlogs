Baby Update

Fetal development this week

Week 24

Your baby is now about the size of a cantaloupe — around 12 inches long and weighing about 1.3 pounds. Their hearing is now developed enough to recognize your voice from outside the womb.

They're practicing breathing movements, inhaling and exhaling amniotic fluid to prepare their lungs for the outside world. Brain development is happening at a rapid pace this week.

What Mom's Experiencing

Physical and emotional changes this week

  • Back pain increasing

    The growing belly shifts her center of gravity. Gentle stretches and a supportive pillow help.

  • Braxton Hicks may start

    Practice contractions are normal at this stage. Irregular and painless — not true labor.

  • Sleep becoming harder

    Finding a comfortable position gets trickier. A full-body pregnancy pillow can be a game-changer.

  • Glucose screening test This week

    Checks for gestational diabetes. She'll drink a sweet solution and have blood drawn one hour later.

Your Focus This Week

Three things that move the needle as a dad

For Dad

Start talking and reading to your baby

They can hear you now. Read a book aloud, tell them about your day, or sing a song. It sounds silly — it's not.

🗣️

Research childbirth classes together

In-person classes fill up fast. Look at hospital-based vs. independent options. Lamaze and Bradley are the most common.

📚

Review your insurance coverage for delivery

Call your insurer and confirm: in-network hospital, OB coverage, and what you'll owe after delivery. No surprises.

Relationship Check-In

One practical thing you can do today

Ask your partner how they're feeling about the upcoming glucose test. Offer to go with them to the appointment — even waiting together makes a difference. The test itself takes about an hour.

Tip: Lead with curiosity, not solutions. "How are you feeling?" is more powerful than "Here's what you should do."
