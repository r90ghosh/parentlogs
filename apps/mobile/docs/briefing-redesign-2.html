Briefing — light & calm, as a digest

A real break from the dark editorial app: light background, soft modern sans-serif, almost no color, and a feed of skimmable one-liners instead of long scrolling sections — the whole week readable in ~20 seconds. The DO items are checkable, so the briefing doubles as this week's action list. Tap any row to expand the detail; tap a circle to check it off.

Week 24
Second trimester · 16 weeks to go
Baby hits a real milestone — and it's nursery season.
This week
Baby
Baby can hear your voice now — and is starting to respond.

Around week 24 baby crosses the “viability” line as the lungs develop. The inner ear is finished and baby is ~12 in and 1.3 lb. Talking and reading out loud now genuinely registers.

Her body
Her center of gravity is shifting — backaches are normal.

Braxton Hicks (practice contractions) may start: tightening that comes and goes. Heartburn and swollen ankles are common too. Nothing alarming — just the second-trimester load.

Do this
Start the nursery — furniture lead times run 8–12 weeks.

Cribs, gliders and dressers routinely take 2–3 months to ship. Picking now means it arrives before the third-trimester scramble.

Do this
Go to the glucose screening with her (it's a 1–2 hr visit).

It screens for gestational diabetes. It's long and a little tedious — good one to show up for.

Tip
Take one thing fully off her plate — without being asked.

Don't announce it. Just handle it. The not-asking is the whole point this week.

Field note
“I recorded myself reading the same short book every night at week 24. After birth, it was the one thing that calmed her at 3 a.m.”
Coming up
Third trimester starts in ~2 weeks. Tour hospitals soon.

Weeks 26–28 bring the third trimester and your glucose results. Start a registry before appointments ramp up.
