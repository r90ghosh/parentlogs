Anxiety · 0–3 months

3 AM and you're wondering if you're cut out for this

There's a specific flavor of despair that only hits at 3 AM when you're holding a screaming baby, you've tried every trick you know, and your brain whispers: maybe I'm not built for this. Every new dad has that moment. Every single one. The dads who seem like they have it together? They've had it too. They just don't post about it.
What to do
1
Set up sleep shifts
Even one 4-hour uninterrupted block per night makes a dramatic difference in mental health.
2
Do the hard reps
Take diaper changes and soothing solo. The discomfort is temporary; the confidence is permanent.
3
Check in with yourself honestly
Persistently hopeless or angry beyond exhaustion? Paternal PPD is real and treatable — talk to your doctor.
From the trenches
"Week two, I was on the nursery floor at 4 AM, baby screaming, and I started crying too. My wife found us both crying. She said, 'Welcome to parenthood.' Somehow that helped."
— Jason, 33, dad of one, New York
"I didn't tell anyone how dark my thoughts got. I wish I had. My doctor said, 'You should have called me on day one.' Got help, got better."
— Brian, 38, dad of two, Minnesota
