Baby Update

Your baby is now about the size of a plum! All the major organs and body systems have formed and will continue to develop throughout the pregnancy. This week, baby's reflexes are kicking in‚Äîquite literally. They can now open and close their fists, curl their toes, and even make sucking movements.

The face is looking more human as the eyes have moved closer together. The kidneys are starting to produce urine, and the intestines, which had been growing in the umbilical cord, are moving into the abdomen.

üí° Miscarriage risk drops to about 2% after seeing a heartbeat at 12 weeks. This is why many couples choose to announce after this milestone.

What Mom's Experiencing

The placenta has now taken over hormone production from the ovaries. This transition often means relief from the most intense first-trimester symptoms. Many women notice their nausea finally starting to ease this week.

Energy levels may start to improve, though some fatigue can persist. She might notice a tiny bump forming, especially if this isn't her first pregnancy. The uterus has grown to about the size of a grapefruit and is starting to rise out of the pelvis.

Emotionally, reaching this milestone can bring relief and allow her to finally feel excited about the pregnancy. It's okay to celebrate!

Relationship Check-In

You've made it through the hardest trimester together. Take a moment to acknowledge that. The exhaustion, the nausea, the anxiety‚Äîyou've supported her through it, and she's grown an actual human.

Tonight's conversation starter: "How are you feeling about telling people? Who should we tell first, and how do you want to do it?"

Planning the announcement together can be a bonding experience. Some couples prefer individual calls to close family, others do a group text, and some go all-out with creative social media reveals. There's no right answer‚Äîdo what feels right for you both.

Coming Up

Week 13: Welcome to the second trimester! Often called the "honeymoon phase" of pregnancy. Energy returns, nausea fades, and the bump starts to show.

Week 14-16: Baby will start moving (though she probably won't feel it until weeks 18-20). Time to start thinking about the anatomy scan coming up around week 20.

On the horizon: Gender reveal possibilities, baby registry planning, and the exciting mid-pregnancy period where she'll actually feel good.
